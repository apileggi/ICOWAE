Study Overview:

You are invited to take part in a study that intends to investigate how individuals respond to specific characteristics of displayed items. To do this, an item will be displayed to you and you will be asked to make a keyboard response according to a specified feature of the item, i.e., either the colour of the item or whether the item is an English word.

 

Participation in this study is voluntary and will take approximately 30 minutes of your time. You will receive 0.5 participation credits towards your psychology courses. During this online study, you can withdraw at any time without loss of participation credit by progressing to the end without submitting your answers. However, once data is collected, please note that researchers will have no way of knowing which data belongs to which participant due to the de-identification of data. The risks associated with participation in the study are expected to be no greater than what you might experience in your day-to-day life.

You will be completing the study in a web browser that uses software created by our lab. The study is hosted on secure Waterloo Arts Research servers and is entirely self-contained so you will not have to download anything to your computer. When information is transmitted over the internet privacy cannot be guaranteed. There is always a risk your responses may be intercepted by a third party (e.g., government agencies, hackers).

University of Waterloo researchers will not collect or use IP addresses or other information which could link your participation to your computer or electronic device without first informing you. Your identity in this study will be de-identified; your ID number will not be included or in any other way associated with the data collected in the study. All de-identified data will be securely stored on password-protected computers, on secure servers at the University of Waterloo and, as needed, on encrypted external hard drives for a minimum of seven (7) years. Furthermore, copies of de-identified data may be posted to an online repository to support the ongoing reproducibility effort within psychological science.

 

This study has been reviewed and received ethics clearance through a University of Waterloo Office Research Ethics Committee. However, the final decision about participation is yours. If you have any comments or concerns resulting from your participation in this study, please contact the Office of Research Ethics at 1-519-888-4567 ext. 36005 or ore-ceo@uwaterloo.ca.

 

Thank you for your participation in this project. If you have any questions about this study, please feel free to contact the student investigator or a faculty supervisor listed at the top of this letter.
